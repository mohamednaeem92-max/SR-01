# SR-01: PROSPERO Registration Ready Summary

## Review Title

Surgical stabilization versus non-operative management of rib fractures in older adults: a systematic review and meta-analysis

## Review Question

In adults aged 65 years and older with traumatic rib fractures, does surgical stabilization (rib fixation) compared to non-operative management reduce mortality and improve clinical outcomes?

## Condition

Traumatic rib fractures in older adults (≥65 years)

## Intervention(s)

Surgical stabilization of rib fractures (SSRF), including:
- Open reduction internal fixation with plates and screws
- Intramedullary fixation
- K-wire fixation
- Any other surgical rib fixation technique

## Comparator(s)/Control

Non-operative management including:
- Oral and intravenous analgesics
- Regional anesthesia (epidural, paravertebral, intercostal, serratus anterior plane blocks)
- Incentive spirometry
- Noninvasive positive pressure ventilation
- Chest physiotherapy
- Multimodal analgesia protocols

## Main Outcome(s)

**Primary outcome:**
- Mortality (in-hospital or within 30 days)

**Secondary outcomes:**
- Pneumonia incidence
- Intensive care unit length of stay (days)
- Hospital length of stay (days)
- Duration of mechanical ventilation (days)
- Tracheostomy rate
- Pain scores (at any reported time point)
- Quality of life / functional outcomes
- Surgical complications (wound infection, implant failure, bleeding, re-operation)

## Population

Adults ≥65 years of age presenting to the emergency department or hospital with traumatic rib fracture(s) of any number, mechanism (blunt or penetrating), and severity (including flail chest).

## Eligibility Criteria

**Inclusion:**
- Age ≥65 years
- Traumatic rib fracture(s)
- Comparative study (surgical vs. non-operative management)
- Reports ≥1 outcome of interest
- Original research: RCT, cohort, case-control, before-after design
- ≥10 participants
- English language

**Exclusion:**
- Age <65 without separate data for ≥65
- Pathologic/non-traumatic fractures
- Single-arm surgical series without comparator
- Case reports, reviews, editorials, animal/cadaver studies
- Non-English publications

## Information Sources

- MEDLINE (PubMed)
- EMBASE
- Cochrane Central Register of Controlled Trials (CENTRAL)
- Web of Science
- ClinicalTrials.gov (for ongoing/completed trials)
- Reference lists of included studies and relevant reviews

## Planned Search Date

To be determined (Phase 3)

## Data Extraction

Two reviewers will independently extract data using a standardized form. Disagreements resolved by consensus or third reviewer.

## Risk of Bias Assessment

- RCTs: Cochrane Risk of Bias 2 (RoB 2) tool
- Observational studies: ROBINS-I tool

## Synthesis

Random-effects meta-analysis using inverse variance method (DerSimonian-Laird) if ≥2 studies with comparable populations and outcomes are available. Heterogeneity assessed by I² statistic and Cochrane Q test. Where meta-analysis is not possible, narrative synthesis will be performed.

## Subgroup Analyses

1. Age: 65–74 vs. 75–84 vs. ≥85 years
2. Fracture pattern: flail chest vs. non-flail multiple fractures vs. single fracture
3. Timing of surgery: early (<72 hours) vs. late (≥72 hours)
4. Study design: RCTs vs. observational

## Sensitivity Analyses

- Excluding studies at high/critical risk of bias
- Excluding abstracts/unpublished data
- Fixed-effects vs. random-effects models
