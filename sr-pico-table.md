# SR-01: PICO Table and Eligibility Criteria

## Research Question

In adults aged 65 years and older with traumatic rib fractures, does surgical stabilization (SSRF/rib fixation) compared to non-operative management reduce mortality and improve clinical outcomes?

## PICO Table

| Element | Description | Inclusion Criteria | Exclusion Criteria |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Population** | Adults ≥65 years with traumatic rib fractures (any number, any mechanism) | ≥65 years; any number of rib fractures (single or multiple); any mechanism (blunt or penetrating) | Age <65; pathologic fractures; non-traumatic fractures (e.g., stress fractures) |
| **Intervention** | Surgical stabilization of rib fractures (any technique) | Open reduction internal fixation (ORIF) with plates/screws; intramedullary devices; K-wires; any surgical rib fixation technique | Rib fixation performed for non-traumatic indications |
| **Comparator** | Non-operative management | Standard medical care including any combination of: oral/IV analgesics, regional anesthesia (epidural, paravertebral, nerve blocks), incentive spirometry, noninvasive ventilation, chest physiotherapy | No comparator group (single-arm surgical series without control) |
| **Outcome (Primary)** | Mortality | In-hospital mortality or 30-day mortality | Mortality reported only as long-term (>1 year) without short-term data |
| **Outcome (Secondary)** | Pneumonia; ICU length of stay; hospital length of stay; duration of mechanical ventilation; tracheostomy rate; pain scores; quality of life/functional outcomes; surgical complications (infection, implant failure, bleeding) | Quantitatively reported with sufficient data for extraction | Qualitative descriptions only |
| **Timeframe** | In-hospital or within 30 days | Any follow-up duration | — |
| **Study Design** | Randomized controlled trials; prospective cohort; retrospective cohort; case-control; before-after studies with ≥10 patients | Comparative studies with both surgical and non-operative groups | Case reports (n<10); narrative reviews; editorials; commentaries; cadaveric/animal studies; conference abstracts without full text |
| **Setting** | Emergency department + hospital (trauma centers, academic or community) | ED presentation followed by inpatient management | Pre-hospital only studies |

## Eligibility Criteria Summary

### Inclusion
1. Adults ≥65 years of age
2. Traumatic rib fracture(s) (any number, any mechanism)
3. Comparison of surgical stabilization vs. non-operative management
4. Reports at least one outcome of interest (mortality, pneumonia, LOS, etc.)
5. Original research (RCT, cohort, case-control, before-after)

### Exclusion
1. Age <65 years (or no subgroup data for ≥65)
2. Non-traumatic rib fractures (pathologic, stress)
3. Single-arm surgical series without non-operative comparator
4. Case reports, reviews, editorials, animal/cadaver studies
5. Non-English full text
6. Duplicate publications of the same cohort (use most complete/recent)

## FINER Criteria Check

| Criterion | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Feasible** | ✅ ~15–25 studies expected. Multiple large NTDB/NSQIP analyses exist with age-stratified data. Single-center cohorts also available. |
| **Interesting** | ✅ Elderly population is the fastest-growing trauma demographic. Clinical equipoise exists regarding whether surgical risks outweigh benefits in older patients. |
| **Novel** | ✅ No systematic review has focused specifically on SSRF outcomes in patients ≥65. Existing SRs (2024, 2025) cover all adults with only subgroup analyses for age. EAST 2023 guideline on elderly explicitly excluded surgical management. |
| **Ethical** | ✅ Systematic review of published literature — no ethical concerns. |
| **Relevant** | ✅ Will directly inform surgical decision-making, risk stratification, and guideline development for geriatric trauma patients with rib fractures. |

## Subgroup Analyses (Planned)

1. Age bands: 65–74 vs. 75–84 vs. ≥85
2. Fracture pattern: flail chest vs. non-flail multiple rib fractures vs. single rib fracture
3. Timing: early surgery (<72 hours) vs. late surgery (≥72 hours)
4. Study design: RCTs vs. observational studies (sensitivity analysis)
5. Mechanism: blunt vs. penetrating
